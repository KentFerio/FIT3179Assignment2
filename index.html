Australia’s Life Expectancy in a Global Context

This dashboard explores Australia’s life expectancy and health outcomes in comparison with global patterns. It begins with a worldwide overview before zooming in to examine differences across Australian states and territories.

Why Compare Australia with the World?

Australia consistently ranks among the countries with the highest life expectancy. Understanding how it compares to global averages helps highlight the factors that contribute to this strong performance — from economic growth to health system stability.

Global Life Expectancy Overview

The choropleth map highlights life expectancy worldwide. Darker blues indicate higher values, while grey areas represent missing data. Australia and Western Europe consistently appear among the top-performing regions from 2011–2020.

Global Economic Development vs Life Expectancy

Key Global Insights

  • Positive Relationship: Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have longer life expectancy.
  • Australia’s Position: Australia sits high on both scales, reflecting strong healthcare and living standards.
  • Regional Gaps: Developing regions in Africa and Asia still lag behind in health outcomes despite gradual improvements.

Life Expectancy Across Australian States and Territories

The chart below shows life expectancy trends across Australian states and territories from 2011 to 2020.

The chart shows a steady rise in life expectancy across most Australian states and territories from 2011 to 2020. Overall, Australia’s health outcomes continue to improve year by year, with the Australian Capital Territory and Victoria maintaining the highest averages above 83 years. However, the Northern Territory lags behind the rest of the country — remaining below 80 years on average. This gap can be linked to factors such as limited healthcare access, higher rural and Indigenous populations, and socioeconomic inequalities. Let’s explore the next visualisations to understand how health spending and funding sources contribute to these differences.

Health Expenditure in Australia

Here are two visualisations that shows the health expenditure on each states and where the funds are coming from

Health Expenditure

Funding Source

The health expenditure chart highlights clear funding disparities between states and territories. New South Wales and Victoria receive the highest health spending, while the Northern Territory consistently shows the lowest expenditure, aligning with its lower life expectancy trend observed earlier. This suggests a possible correlation between total funding and health outcomes. On the right, the funding source breakdown shows that most health expenditure is financed by government sources, particularly the Australian Government and state governments. In contrast, non-government contributions — such as health insurance funds and individual spending — make up a smaller proportion, illustrating Australia’s strong public healthcare funding structure.

Average Annual Temperature in Australia

This chart shows how Australia’s average annual temperature anomaly has changed since 1910, compared to the 1961–90 baseline. Rising anomalies highlight a clear warming trend in recent decades.

Over the past decade, Australia’s mean temperature has increased by 1°C, reflecting a steady warming trend that aligns with global climate change patterns. This rise in temperature can have direct and indirect effects on health outcomes — increasing the frequency of heat-related illnesses, dehydration, and cardiovascular stress, particularly among vulnerable populations such as the elderly and those in rural or remote regions. As Australia continues to experience hotter and more extreme conditions, it raises an important question: how long can life expectancy continue to rise if climate-related health risks become more prevalent? The data suggests that while medical and healthcare improvements have boosted longevity, environmental challenges may increasingly offset these gains in the coming decades.
